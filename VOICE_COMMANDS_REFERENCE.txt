BASIC FORMAT:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
"[PARTY_NAME] [MATERIAL] [VEHICLE] [QUANTITY] [TRIPS] [RATE] [PAYMENT]"


📋 COMMAND EXAMPLES
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

EXAMPLE 1 - Basic Transaction:
────────────────────────────────
Say: "Ramesh reti tractor 6 1200 udhar"
Extracted:
  • Party: Ramesh
  • Material: reti (sand)
  • Vehicle: tractor
  • Quantity: 6 tons
  • Rate: ₹1200 per ton
  • Payment: udhar (pending/credit)
  • Total Amount: ₹7,200

EXAMPLE 2 - With Trips:
──────────────────────
Say: "Amit balu truck 10 trip 800 paid"
Extracted:
  • Party: Amit
  • Material: balu (sand)
  • Vehicle: truck
  • Quantity: 10 tons
  • Trips: 1 trip
  • Rate: ₹800 per ton
  • Payment: paid (cash)
  • Total Amount: ₹8,000

EXAMPLE 3 - Partial Payment:
────────────────────────────
Say: "Priya stone tractor 5 1500 advance"
Extracted:
  • Party: Priya
  • Material: stone
  • Vehicle: tractor
  • Quantity: 5 tons
  • Rate: ₹1500 per ton
  • Payment: advance (partial)
  • Total Amount: ₹7,500

EXAMPLE 4 - Multiple Trips:
─────────────────────────────
Say: "Sharma mitti tempo 20 5 trip 600 udhar"
Extracted:
  • Party: Sharma
  • Material: mitti (soil)
  • Vehicle: tempo
  • Quantity: 20 units
  • Trips: 5
  • Rate: ₹600 per unit
  • Payment: udhar (pending)
  • Total Amount: ₹12,000

EXAMPLE 5 - No Vehicle:
──────────────────────────
Say: "Suresh cement 8 2500 paid"
Extracted:
  • Party: Suresh
  • Material: cement
  • Vehicle: (not specified)
  • Quantity: 8 bags
  • Rate: ₹2500 per bag
  • Payment: paid
  • Total Amount: ₹20,000

💰 PAYMENT STATUS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

UDHAR (PENDING - CREDIT):
  Say: "udhar", "उधार", "credit", "क्रेडिट"
  Status: Pending (not paid yet, customer owes money)
  Use: when customer takes goods on credit

PAID (CASH/IMMEDIATE):
  Say: "paid", "पेइड", "cash", "नगद", "pay"
  Status: Paid (customer paid in full)
  Use: when customer pays immediately

ADVANCE (PARTIAL - PARTIAL PAYMENT):
  Say: "advance", "एडवांस", "partial", "आंशिक"
  Status: Partial (customer paid some, rest pending)
  Use: when customer pays partial amount


📊 QUANTITY FORMATS (All Work)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  6 tons      → quantity: 6
  6 tonne     → quantity: 6
  six tons    → quantity: 6 (if recognized)
  20 bags     → quantity: 20
  10 units    → quantity: 10
  2.5 tons    → quantity: 2.5 (decimal works)


⏱️ TRIP DETAILS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

OPTIONAL:
  • If not mentioned: Assumed 1 trip
  • If mentioned: "6 trip" = 6 trips

EXAMPLES:
  "Ramesh reti tractor 10 trip 800" 
    → 10 tons in 1 trip, not 10 trips!
    
  "Amit balu truck 20 5 trip 600"
    → 20 tons, 5 trips, ₹600 per ton
    
  "Priya stone 15 3 trip 1500"
    → 15 tons in 3 trips, ₹1500 per ton


💡 QUICK TIPS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

TIP 1: Clear Speech
  Speak slowly and clearly
  Pause between different fields
  Avoid background noise

TIP 2: Order Matters
  Party → Material → Vehicle → Quantity → Trips → Rate → Payment
  Try to maintain this order for better parsing

TIP 3: Common Mistakes to Avoid
  ✗ "Ramesh 6 reti tractor 1200" (wrong order)
  ✓ "Ramesh reti tractor 6 1200" (correct)
  
  ✗ "Ramesh sand tractor six tons 1200" (too verbose)
  ✓ "Ramesh sand tractor 6 1200" (simple)

TIP 4: If Parser Fails
  • Click "Clear" and try again
  • Speak slower and clearer
  • Simplify your command
  • Add partial command first, edit in form

TIP 5: No Vehicle?
  No problem! Skip and go to material/quantity/rate
  "Ramesh cement 8 2500 paid" (no vehicle)
